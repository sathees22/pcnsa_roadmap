01

Foundations & Prerequisites

Week 1–2 · Build your networking base before diving into PAN-OS

Beginner
Networking Fundamentals Review
OSI model, TCP/IP, subnetting (CIDR), VLANs, routing basics (static & dynamic), NAT. You MUST be solid here — PCNSA assumes this knowledge.
⏱ 1 Week 📖 Theory 🔴 Critical
Cybersecurity Basics
Understand firewalls, IDS/IPS, VPNs, threat types (malware, phishing, DoS), encryption concepts (TLS, SSL), and Zero Trust principles.
⏱ 3 Days 📖 Theory 🔴 Critical
Palo Alto Ecosystem Overview
Learn Palo Alto's product family: PA-Series (hardware), VM-Series, CN-Series, Panorama, Prisma Access, Cortex XDR. Understand how PCNSA fits in the certification path.
⏱ 2 Days 📖 Theory
02

PAN-OS Architecture & Initial Setup

Week 2–3 · Learn the core architecture of Palo Alto firewalls

Core
PAN-OS Architecture Deep Dive
Single-pass parallel processing (SP3), data plane vs management plane separation, App-ID, User-ID, Content-ID, Device-ID — the four core identification engines.
⏱ 3 Days 📚 Study 🔴 Critical
Initial Firewall Configuration
Management interface setup, admin accounts, licensing, software updates, commit/validate process, basic system settings (DNS, NTP, hostname).
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab 🔴 Critical
Interface Types & Zones
Layer 2, Layer 3, Virtual Wire, Tap, HA interfaces. Security zones concept — Trust, Untrust, DMZ. Understand intrazone vs interzone traffic.
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab 🔴 Critical
Routing & Virtual Routers
Configure static routes, virtual routers, route redistribution, ECMP. Understand how PAN-OS routes traffic between zones.
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab
NAT Policies
Source NAT (SNAT), Destination NAT (DNAT), U-Turn NAT, No-NAT rules. Configure NAT for internet access and port forwarding scenarios.
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab 🔴 Critical
03

Security Policies & App-ID

Week 4–5 · Master policy creation — the heart of PCNSA

Core
Security Policy Fundamentals
Policy rule components (source/dest zone, address, app, service, action), rule ordering, shadowed rules, default deny-all, intrazone default allow.
⏱ 3 Days 🖥️ Lab 🔴 Critical
App-ID Deep Dive
How App-ID identifies applications, application signatures, App-ID updates, unknown-tcp/udp, application dependencies, application groups and filters.
⏱ 2 Days 📚 Study 🔴 Critical
User-ID Configuration
Map IP to users via Active Directory, LDAP, syslog, XML API. Captive Portal authentication, group mapping, User-ID agents, terminal services agent.
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab 🟡 Important
Address Objects & Groups
IP/Netmask, IP Range, FQDN, dynamic address groups. Tags and dynamic tagging. How to use them effectively in security policies.
⏱ 1 Day 🖥️ Lab
Service Objects & Groups
Custom service objects (TCP/UDP ports), service groups, application-default vs any. When to use application-default vs specific ports.
⏱ 1 Day 🖥️ Lab
04

Security Profiles & Threat Prevention

Week 6–7 · Content-ID, SSL Inspection, WildFire

Advanced
Antivirus & Anti-Spyware Profiles
Configure Antivirus profiles (stream-based scanning), Anti-Spyware profiles for C2 detection, DNS sinkholing, botnet reports. Attach profiles to security rules.
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab 🔴 Critical
Vulnerability Protection Profiles
Protect against exploits with vulnerability protection. Custom threat signatures, exception handling, severity-based actions (alert, drop, reset).
⏱ 1 Day 🖥️ Lab 🔴 Critical
URL Filtering
PAN-DB URL categories, custom URL categories, block/alert/allow/continue/override actions. Safe search enforcement, log settings.
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab 🔴 Critical
File Blocking & WildFire
File blocking profiles, WildFire cloud analysis for unknown files, WildFire subscription, verdicts (benign/grayware/malware), WildFire real-time signatures.
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab 🟡 Important
SSL/TLS Decryption
Forward proxy vs inbound inspection, decryption profiles, SSL exclusions, certificate management, SSL decryption policy creation. Privacy considerations.
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab 🔴 Critical
DoS & Zone Protection
Zone protection profiles, DoS protection policies, flood protection (SYN, UDP, ICMP), reconnaissance protection, packet-based attack protection.
⏱ 1 Day 📚 Study
05

VPN, Logging & Panorama

Week 8–9 · Remote access, visibility, and centralized management

Advanced
IPSec VPN (Site-to-Site)
IKE Phase 1 & Phase 2, tunnel interfaces, crypto profiles (IKE & IPSec), route-based vs policy-based VPN, monitoring tunnel status.
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab 🔴 Critical
GlobalProtect VPN (Remote Access)
GlobalProtect gateway/portal configuration, authentication profiles, agent settings, split tunneling, host information profile (HIP).
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab 🔴 Critical
Logging & Monitoring
Traffic, threat, URL, WildFire logs. Log forwarding to Syslog/SNMP/Email. Log profiles, Custom log formats. ACC (Application Command Center) dashboard.
⏱ 2 Days 🖥️ Lab 🟡 Important
Panorama Centralized Management
Panorama architecture, device groups, templates, template stacks, shared policies, log collector, push configuration, managed devices overview.
⏱ 2 Days 📚 Study 🟡 Important
High Availability (HA)
Active/Passive and Active/Active HA modes, HA1/HA2/HA3 links, preemption, failover triggers, session synchronization, HA monitoring.
⏱ 1 Day 📚 Study
06

Exam Preparation & Practice

Week 10–13 · Review, practice exams, and final prep

Final
Full Topic Review & Gap Analysis
Review all exam objectives from Palo Alto's official study guide. Identify weak areas and create focused review sessions for each. Prioritize heavily tested topics.
⏱ 1 Week 📖 Review 🔴 Critical
Palo Alto EDU-110 Official Training
Complete the official Firewall Essentials (EDU-110) course from Palo Alto Networks. Free digital learning path available on the Beacon learning platform.
⏱ 1 Week 🎓 Course 🔴 Critical
Hands-On Lab Practice (PANOS VM)
Use PAN-OS VM-50 (free trial), GNS3/EVE-NG, or Palo Alto's learning labs. Practice every topic with real config. Focus on NAT, security policies, and SSL decryption labs.
⏱ 2 Weeks 🖥️ Lab 🔴 Critical
Practice Exams & Question Banks
Use Boson, MeasureUp, or ExamTopics for practice questions. Target 80%+ consistently before scheduling the real exam. Review all incorrect answers carefully.
⏱ 1 Week ✍️ Practice 🔴 Critical
Schedule & Take the Exam
Register via Pearson VUE (in-person) or OnVUE (online proctored). Take exam early in the day when focused. Trust your prep — you've got this! 🎯
⏱ Day of 🏆 Exam 🔴 Final Step
